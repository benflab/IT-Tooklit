IT Policy is the set of rules and regulations put in place to ensure IT activites comply with the regulations for a given field.

IT Policy is important because it defines the boundries that IT activities must stay within and the rules that must be followed for the successful execution of IT systems. If IT policy remained undefined, IT operations could potentially violate goverment regulation, expose companies or groups to risks of many types, and minimize any kind of assurance plans in play. IT Policy is more general than IT governace, IT risk, and IT strategy--In fact IT Policy covers all of those fields.

IT Policy Challenges

Creating IT policies in a changing environment is not a straightforward task but often a necessary one. Organizations might not fully appreciate advantages, limitations and risk factors of emerging technologies; for instance, choosing a cloud computing solution requires management of the associated risk and empowerment for a route to create business value in an environment full of uncertainties.
